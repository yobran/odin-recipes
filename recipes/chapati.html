Chapati

Description

Chapati is a type of unleavened flatbread that is a staple in East African cuisine.

It is made from simple ingredients and cooked on a hot griddle, resulting in a soft and slightly chewy texture.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons oil or melted butter

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add water and oil, mixing until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  6. On a floured surface, roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter.
  7. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium-high heat. Cook each chapati for about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until golden brown spots appear.
  8. Remove from heat and keep warm. Serve hot with your favorite dishes.
